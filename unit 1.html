Introduction to Computers

A computer is an electronic device that processes data to produce meaningful information.

Key Features

  • Accuracy: Computers provide accurate results.
  • Speed: They operate at high speeds.
  • Storage: Computers can store large amounts of data.
  • Versatility: They can perform various tasks.
  • Diligence: Computers don't tire and can work continuously.
  • Automatic: They perform tasks automatically once instructed.

Applications

  • Education
  • Communication
  • Business
  • Engineering
  • Science and Research

Advantages

  • Large data handling
  • Fast processing
  • Efficient communication

Disadvantages

  • Cannot make independent decisions
  • Requires power to operate
  • Can cause eye strain with prolonged use

Generations of Computers

  1. First Generation (1946-1958): Vacuum tubes
  2. Second Generation (1959-1964): Transistors
  3. Third Generation (1965-1974): Integrated Circuits (ICs)
  4. Fourth Generation (1975-1990): VLSI Microprocessors
  5. Fifth Generation (1990-Present): ULSI, Artificial Intelligence
